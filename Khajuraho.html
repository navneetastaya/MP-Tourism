Top 10 Places to Visit in Khajuraho

Parsvanath-Temple

The Parsvanath Temple, one of the most revered Jain temples in Khajuraho, stands as a testament to the city's rich cultural heritage and architectural brilliance. Built in the 10th century, this temple is dedicated to Lord Parsvanath, the 23rd Tirthankara of Jainism. Its intricately carved sculptures, depicting various Jain deities and mythical creatures, are a marvel of craftsmanship. Visitors can admire the temple's serene ambiance, intricate detailing, and spiritual significance, making it a must-visit destination for history enthusiasts and religious pilgrims alike.

Khajuraho Temples

The Khajuraho Temples, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, are a stunning testament to India's rich architectural and cultural heritage. Dating back to the Chandela dynasty from the 10th to 12th centuries, these temples are renowned for their exquisite carvings, intricate sculptures, and erotic art. Visitors can explore the Western Group of Temples, which includes iconic structures like the Kandariya Mahadev Temple and the Lakshmana Temple, marveling at their architectural grandeur and spiritual significance. The Khajuraho Temples are not just architectural wonders but also a symbol of India's artistic and cultural legacy.

Devi Jagdamba Temple

The Devi Jagdamba Temple, dedicated to the Hindu goddess Devi Jagdamba, is a magnificent shrine located in the Western Group of Temples in Khajuraho. Built in the 12th century, the temple is renowned for its impressive architecture, intricate carvings, and spiritual significance. Visitors can admire the temple's ornate facade, adorned with sculptures of celestial beings, mythical creatures, and divine motifs, reflecting the rich symbolism of Hindu mythology. The Devi Jagdamba Temple is a sacred pilgrimage site and a testament to Khajuraho's architectural splendor and religious devotion.

Lakshmana Temple

The Lakshmana Temple, one of the most prominent structures in the Western Group of Temples in Khajuraho, is dedicated to the Hindu deity Lord Vishnu. Built in the 10th century, this temple is renowned for its exquisite architecture, intricate carvings, and spiritual significance. Visitors can admire the temple's towering shikhara, adorned with sculptures of gods, goddesses, and celestial beings, depicting scenes from Hindu mythology. The Lakshmana Temple is a masterpiece of Chandela architecture and a symbol of Khajuraho's rich cultural heritage.

Khajuraho's Textile & Looms

Khajuraho's Textile & Looms industry is renowned for its exquisite craftsmanship, intricate designs, and vibrant colors. The town's skilled artisans create a wide range of textiles, including sarees, dupattas, and fabrics, using traditional techniques passed down through generations. Visitors can explore Khajuraho's bustling markets, interact with local weavers, and purchase authentic handloom products as souvenirs. The textiles of Khajuraho are not just garments but also a reflection of the town's rich cultural heritage and artistic legacy.

Kandariya Mahadev Temple

The Kandariya Mahadev Temple, the largest and most ornate temple in Khajuraho, is dedicated to Lord Shiva. Built in the 11th century, this temple is renowned for its towering shikhara, intricate carvings, and spiritual significance. Visitors can admire the temple's elaborate facade, adorned with sculptures of gods, goddesses, and mythical creatures, depicting scenes from Hindu mythology. The Kandariya Mahadev Temple is a masterpiece of Chandela architecture and a symbol of Khajuraho's rich cultural heritage and religious devotion.

Dulhadev Temple

The Dulhadev Temple, located in the Western Group of Temples in Khajuraho, is dedicated to the Hindu deity Lord Shiva. Built in the 12th century, this temple is renowned for its exquisite architecture, intricate carvings, and spiritual significance. Visitors can admire the temple's elegant design, adorned with sculptures of gods, goddesses, and celestial beings, depicting scenes from Hindu mythology. The Dulhadev Temple is a testament to Khajuraho's rich cultural heritage and religious devotion.

Chaturbhuj Temple

The Chaturbhuj Temple, dedicated to the Hindu deity Lord Vishnu, is an ancient shrine located in the Eastern Group of Temples in Khajuraho. Built in the 12th century, this temple is renowned for its simple yet elegant architecture and spiritual significance. Visitors can admire the temple's serene ambiance, ornate carvings, and sacred rituals, making it a popular pilgrimage site for devotees and tourists alike.

Varaha Temple

The Varaha Temple, dedicated to the Hindu deity Varaha, is an ancient shrine located in the Western Group of Temples in Khajuraho. Built in the 10th century, this temple is renowned for its exquisite architecture, intricate carvings, and spiritual significance. Visitors can admire the temple's majestic facade, adorned with sculptures of Varaha, the boar incarnation of Lord Vishnu, and other mythical creatures, depicting scenes from Hindu mythology.

Matangeshwara Temple

The Matangeshwara Temple, dedicated to the Hindu deity Lord Shiva, is a sacred shrine located in the Western Group of Temples in Khajuraho. Built in the 9th century, this temple is renowned for its impressive architecture, intricate carvings, and spiritual significance. Visitors can admire the temple's towering shikhara, adorned with sculptures of Shiva and other deities, and witness the sacred rituals and ceremonies that take place within its sanctum sanctorum.

Viswanath Temple

The Viswanath Temple, located in the Western Group of Temples in Khajuraho, is a sacred shrine dedicated to the Hindu deity Lord Shiva. Built in the 11th century, this temple is renowned for its exquisite architecture, intricate carvings, and spiritual significance. Visitors can admire the temple's ornate facade, adorned with sculptures of gods, goddesses, and celestial beings, depicting scenes from Hindu mythology. The Viswanath Temple is not just a place of worship but also a symbol of Khajuraho's rich cultural heritage and religious devotion.

Nandi Temple

The Nandi Temple, situated in the Western Group of Temples in Khajuraho, is dedicated to Nandi, the sacred bull and vehicle of the Hindu deity Lord Shiva. Built in the 11th century, this small yet elegant shrine is renowned for its intricate carvings, serene ambiance, and spiritual significance. Visitors can admire the temple's beautifully sculpted Nandi idol, symbolizing strength, devotion, and loyalty, and witness the rituals and ceremonies that take place within its sanctum sanctorum. The Nandi Temple is a sacred pilgrimage site and a testament to Khajuraho's rich cultural heritage and religious traditions.
