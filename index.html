Explore the beauty of central India

Visiting City's & Places

Bhopal

Bhopal, the "City of Lakes", is a captivating blend of natural beauty and historical richness. Embark on a serene journey at the Upper and Lower Lakes, where you can enjoy tranquil boat rides amidst picturesque surroundings. Delve into the city's architectural heritage with a visit to Taj-ul-Masajid, one of the largest mosques in India, renowned for its magnificent domes and intricate craftsmanship. For a cultural immersion, Bharat Bhavan stands as a beacon, showcasing a diverse range of contemporary and traditional art forms, including visual arts, theater, and music.

Previous Next
Indore

Indore, the bustling commercial capital, is a vibrant tapestry of history and modernity. Immerse yourself in the regal ambiance of Rajwada Palace, an architectural marvel adorned with intricate carvings and majestic courtyards, offering a glimpse into the city's royal past. Experience the culinary delights of Sarafa Bazaar, a nocturnal paradise where you can savor an array of delectable street food delicacies amidst the lively atmosphere. Unravel the mysteries of the past at the Central Museum, home to an extensive collection of artifacts, including sculptures, coins, and manuscripts, tracing the rich heritage of Madhya Pradesh through the ages.

Previous Next
Gwalior

Gwalior, the "Fort City", exudes an aura of grandeur and mystique, with its towering forts and palaces steeped in history. Explore the majestic Gwalior Fort, perched atop a steep hill, offering panoramic views of the city and housing a treasure trove of architectural marvels, including palaces, temples, and water tanks. Step into the lap of luxury at Jai Vilas Palace, a magnificent edifice adorned with European-style architecture, opulent furnishings, and an extensive collection of antiques and artifacts. Discover the spiritual resonance of Sas Bahu Temple, an ancient marvel dedicated to Lord Vishnu, featuring exquisite carvings and intricate sculptures that narrate tales of devotion and divine grace.

Previous Next
Orchha

Orchha, nestled on the banks of the serene Betwa River, is a testament to the grandeur of bygone eras, with its majestic forts and temples. Immerse yourself in the architectural splendor of Orchha Fort Complex, a sprawling ensemble of palaces, temples, and cenotaphs, offering a glimpse into the region's rich cultural heritage. Marvel at the intricate frescoes and elaborate architecture of Raj Mahal and Jahangir Mahal, which bear witness to the patronage of the Bundela rulers and their love for art and architecture. Seek solace in the divine ambiance of Chaturbhuj Temple, dedicated to Lord Vishnu, where the echoes of ancient chants resonate through the sacred precincts, invoking a sense of peace and spirituality.

Khajuraho

Khajuraho, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is renowned for its exquisite temples adorned with intricate carvings and sculptures that depict various aspects of human life and spirituality. Embark on a journey through time as you explore the stunning temples of Khajuraho, marveling at the architectural brilliance of structures such as Kandariya Mahadeva Temple and Lakshmana Temple, which stand as enduring symbols of medieval Indian art and craftsmanship. Delve into the spiritual significance of these ancient monuments, where every sculpture tells a story and every carving reflects the divine beauty of creation. Experience the timeless allure of Khajuraho, where the past merges seamlessly with the present, inviting visitors to embark on a voyage of discovery and enlightenment.
